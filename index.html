Doppelkopf

Rules & Trump Order

Goal: Two hidden teams form each round — whoever holds a ♣Q (Queen of Clubs) plays for Re, everyone else plays for Kontra. Re needs 121+ of the 240 card points to win the round; Kontra wins with 120+.

Trump order (highest → lowest): ♥10, ♣Q, ♠Q, ♥Q, ♦Q, ♣J, ♠J, ♥J, ♦J, ♦A, ♦10, ♦K, ♦9. Both ♥10s outrank even the ♣Q. All diamonds are trump, plus the ♥10. Clubs/Spades Aces, 10s, Kings, 9s and Hearts Aces, Kings, 9s are plain suits (rank: A > 10 > K > 9).

Tie-break exception: when two identical cards meet in a trick, the one played first normally wins — except for the two ♥10s, where the second one played beats the first.

Card points: A=11, 10=10, K=4, Q=3, J=2, 9=0 (each card exists twice, 240 points total).

Following suit: You must follow the effective suit led (trump, or a plain suit) if you can. Otherwise you may play anything, including trumping in.

Scoring bonuses: +1 if the losing side scores under 90, +1 more under 60, +1 more under 30, +1 more if they score 0 ("schwarz"). +1 "Doppelkopf" bonus for each trick worth 40+ points, credited to whichever team won it.

Announcements (Ansagen): A player may call "Re" or "Kontra" for their own party while still holding 11+ cards (i.e. up to their own first card). This raises the round's stakes by 2 points, awarded to whichever party ends up winning. Once their party has announced, either member may then escalate with "keine 90" (10+ cards left), "keine 60" (9+), "keine 30" (8+), or "schwarz" (7+) — each predicting the opponents won't reach that many points. A fulfilled Absage earns the winning party +1 extra, but only if they were the ones who called it. Announcing (or playing a ♣Q) reveals that individual player's team.

Note: this implementation plays standard rounds only — solos and marriages ("Hochzeit") are not included.
